Kappa Ceti System

Home to nearly 5 billion people, KCIII (Cylon) was established early in the golden age of space travel. Bulk haulers crowd into the dozen stations in orbit, sending the bounty of the system's asteroid belt down the space elevators to fuel the sprawling metropolis below. Vast regions of the planet are owned by mega wealthy individuals, forcing the majority of the population into the overpopulated capital city of New Shenzhen. Many of these people dream of saving enough for a ticket on a jumpliner to the Anteros system, where they say the asteroids are riddled with gold.

Out on the edge of the system sits a quarantine zone, no one is certain what lies at its centre: a top secret military base, the home world of a sentient alien species, or a corporate research and development facility are all popular theories. Whichever it is, someone with deep pockets is paying for a fleet of Troubleshooters to keep people away from it.

Anteros System

The hyperlane that connects Anteros to Kappa Ceti was discovered over 500 years ago, but before large scale colonisation could kick off the bit rot virus struck, destroying humanity's knowledge of hyperspace travel. 12 years ago explorers found a hyperlane map unleashing a gold rush to the Antoros System.

The largest colony is on Mani, some 5,000 people on a tide locked ocean world. Other notable colonies include the Adoni crater habitation zone and the solarian mission on Afrit, the moon of Mani.

In the system's meteor belt a bevy of small mining operations and other outposts have cropped up. The mining potential of the system's asteroid belt is said to be so high that Greenstar towed an entire station through hyperspace to exploit it, though there are rumours that the station may be under new management.

Samsa System

Samsa is the frontier proper. Out here the ones who make the law are the ones with the biggest guns and quickest draws. Since the system was re-discoverd it seems that every washed up old astronaut in the rim has a story about the time they went on a run to Samsa: Some tell of the desert moon of Karth, and its orbital defenses left over from the first golden age of space travel. Others whisper about a a ship graveyard, floating around a dead planet at the very far edge of the system. Whether these tales are true or not, there's more unclaimed real estate to be explored and exploited in Samsa than just about anywhere else right now.
