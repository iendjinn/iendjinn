Movement (Continued)

Prone: Under certain situations a unit may become prone, this should be indicated with a token or by placing the unit's model on its side. While prone:

  • A unit may expend half its movement to stand up
  • Moving while prone costs 2" of movement for each 1" moved
  • Prone units may not charge

Engaged: A unit is engaged if it has an enemy unit in base contact with it.

Charging: A unit may charge an enemy unit by moving into base contact with it. If it can move into contact with multiple units it may charge more than one enemy unit.

Climbing: Instead of moving a unit may climb up or down a piece of terrain shorter than its movement characteristic. If a piece of terrain is narrower than the unit's base then it may climb up and down as a single climb.

Jumping: When a unit moves, it may move over a gap less than its height.

For rules on falling, pushing, and difficult terrain see advanced movement rules.

Ranged Phase

The player with priority makes ranged attacks with all of their units then the other player does the same.

When a unit makes ranged attacks it must obey the following rules:

  • It must target the closest enemy unit within line of sight, units that are wholly below the base of the unit may be ignored for determining the closest unit
  • A unit cannot make ranged attacks if it is engaged

To make a ranged attack roll a D6 for each attack that the unit's ranged weapon has. If the value is equal or greater than the unit's ranged attribute a hit is scored.

For each hit scored the target unit must make a save, losing a wound if it fails. If a unit loses all of its wounds it is killed in action.

Save: Roll a D6, if the value is equal or greater than the unit's save it passes. If the save is made as a result of a ranged or melee attack then reduce the unit's save by the armor piercing (AP) value of the weapon. E.g. Save 3+ becomes 5+ against an AP2 weapon.

Killed in Action: When a unit is killed in action remove it from the battlefield.

Line of Sight: A unit has line of sight to another unit if a line can be drawn between the two units without passing through a piece of terrain or other unit (including friendly).

  • If line of sight cannot be drawn to the entirety of a target unit is counts as obscured
  • Infantry may ignore other friendly infantry that they are in base contact with when drawing line of sight.

Obscured: If a target is obscured then reduce the ranged skill of the attacking unit by 1. E.g. 4+ becomes 5+.

For rules on cover, multiple attacks, and weapon types see advanced ranged rules.

Melee Phase

The player with priority groups units that are in base contact into melees such that each melee contains at least one of their units and one enemy unit and the number of pairs of units is maximized.

The player with priority then picks melees in turn and they are then resolved by the following steps:

  • Each player totals up the melee attribute for each of their units in the melee and rolls that number of D6 (melee attacks)
  • The player with the highest value wins the melee, in the event of a draw the winner is the side with the unit with the highest single melee attribute (flip a coin if there is still a tie)
  • Each unit on the losing side is pushed 1" back from the winning units in the melee
  • The winning player allocates a number of hits equal to their melee attacks to the losing units
  • For each hit allocated against it, losing units must make a save, losing a wound if they fail. If a unit loses all of its wounds it is killed in action

For rules on prone units in melee see advanced melee rules.
